Ever wonder what happens when you lock two animators in a room with too much coffee and a copy of Tony Hawk Pro Skater 2?

Well, you get Skate Beats. Obviously.

## The "Aha" Moment (or Lack Thereof)

It wasn't some grand epiphany. There was no lightning bolt, no divine intervention. Just Joana screaming "WHAT IF THE GRIND RAILS MOVED TO THE MUSIC?" at 3 AM while I was trying to render a walk cycle.

And honestly? That's pretty much how everything at Miau Studio starts.

## We Know Nothing About Game Dev

Here's the thing – we're animators. We know keyframes, not code. We know timing charts, not tech stacks. We know how to make things look good, not necessarily how to make them... work.

But that's the beauty of it, right?

Every day is a "figure it out" adventure. Why is the skater clipping through the rail? No clue. Why does the neon glitch out when you hit a 900° spin? Who knows! Why did the game just crash and play a distorted version of the menu music?

Feature, not bug.

## The Tony Hawk × Beat Saber Mindset

We grew up on Tony Hawk. We spent countless hours perfecting combos, memorizing levels, and definitely not throwing controllers at walls when we missed the final gap.

And then Beat Saber showed up and ruined our wrists but also showed us something – rhythm games don't have to be just about hitting notes. They can be about *feeling* the music.

So we asked ourselves: **What if Tony Hawk had a baby with a rhythm game?**

Turns out, that's a really fun question to answer.

## Neon-Soaked Streets That Breathe

The core concept – levels that reconstruct themselves to the rhythm – came from us being bored with static environments.

Why should the rail always be in the same place? Why should the gap always be the same distance? Why can't the city *move* with you?

So we built a system that listens to the music and rebuilds the level in real-time. Every run is different. Every combo is unique. Every "OH CRAP" moment is beautifully unpredictable.

## It Never Plays the Same Way Twice

And that's the magic, isn't it?

You could play Skate Beats for a hundred hours and never see the same layout twice. The city becomes this living, breathing thing that dances with you. You're not just skating to the music – you're skating *with* it.

## Coming Soon (We Promise)

We're still in the trenches. Still fixing bugs. Still arguing about whether the skater should have more "swag" in their kickflip animation (spoiler: they will).

But we're getting there. And we can't wait to share this beautiful mess with you.
